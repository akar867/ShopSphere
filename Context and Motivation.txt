Context and Motivation
The UoN Language Learning Web Application is a developed web application for a Master of Information Technology project. The application has been developed using Java. The application is focused on vocabulary acquisition, and as the name suggests, involves the use of multimedia to enhance the vocabulary learning process, where words are presented with audio, images, and video clips. This use of a multimedia approach is based on Mayer (2009) and his theoretical work in the area of multimedia learning, which states that retention of knowledge is better with both visual and auditory prompts. While Java was chosen for the project, other frameworks are now more often seen in educational technology, including .NET and Python/Django. This raises the overarching question of how different frameworks support language learning applications. 

The exploration of different frameworks emerges from more than just academic curiosity; it emerges from the challenges of trying to build the language learning web application. From the beginning of the web application development process, the choice of framework will impact how easy it will be for a web application, like the language learning web application, to support multimedia, support multiple users at the same time, support mobile responsiveness and make it easy to maintain over the long term. 

Research Paper Purpose

This paper provides a critical analysis and topical comparison of web development frameworks, specifically in the context of applications for language education, with specific attention to frameworks based on Java, .NET, or newer frameworks. The paper provides the following:

Review the available research literature which has studied the technical and pedagogical
 aspects of frameworks.

Compare the ways in which different frameworks addressed requirements like multimedia integration, usability, scalability, and security.

Relate findings back to the University of Newcastle project, and further educational contexts.

Research Gap

Previous studies have frequently provided comparisons between frameworks as a result of technical requirements, whereas parameters such as execution speed, consumption of memory, and ease of deployment have been examined (Rahman et al, 2021; Ferdous et al, 2019). There is, however, limited evidence which has correlated framework selection to pedagogical development, particularly in the context of language education and multi-mediated contexts. While there is a strong call for research in the Computer-Assisted Language Learning (CALL) literature, the value of technology in student engagement has been established (Chapelle & Sauro, 2017; Holotescu et al, 2020). However, few if any studies have examined the impact of the capabilities of possible frameworks and frameworks designed to support educational innovation. To address this amended gap, this review seeks to provide a synthesis of accounts within both computing and educational research.
Importance of the Study

This study is important for three reasons. 

It will provide developers with information about the relative trade-offs amongst frameworks, enabling developers to select technology for use in future language learning projects.

It will provide educators data about the impact of technology-related decisions on multimedia use, learner engagement, and accessibility.

It will establish bridges between studies focused on computing frameworks and studies based on teaching and learning in a CALL context.

Outline of the Paper

This paper is structured as follows: Section 2 outlines the method of the research and states the research questions guiding the study. Section 3 reviews the literature and is organized into subsections focused on Java frameworks, .NET frameworks and considerations for new frameworks. Section 4 provides pedagogical implications based on framework choice and discuss the areas of technical and educational considerations. Section 5 concludes the paper with reflections, a set of recommendations, and considerations for future research.

The review simultaneously analyses technical and pedagogical considerations and indicates that framework choice is not solely a technical decision made using software engineering principles, but also a pedagogical decision that will shape the language learning experience of students, or their experience learning the second language.
Research Methodology

The research uses SLR (systematic literature review) method to investigate how web development environments (specifically Java, .NET, and frameworks in the future) support the design of language learning applications that utilize multimedia-based content. SLR provides a framework for evidence collection that is systematic, exhaustive, and reproducible (Kitchenham et al., 2015).

Literature Search Methodology 

The search methodology focused, with few exceptions, on peer-reviewed journal articles and corresponding papers during the time frame of 2015 - 2024. ScienceDirect, IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, SpringerLink, and Google Scholar were the scholarly databases. The some terms and phrases utilized to search include:

“Java frameworks for web development”

“ASP.NET Core language learning application”

“Django educational technology”

“Web frameworks multimedia integration”

“Framework comparison scalability security performance”

“Computer-assisted language learning technology frameworks”

In consideration of the assignment rubric, JSR articles were used for reference in the final synthesis, minus the occasional technical articles (Microsoft, Oracle), by providing a principled and authoritative tool.

Inclusion/Exclusion Criteria

Inclusion: Articles that study frameworks on topics of relation to scalability, usability, multimedia, and educational technology;

Exclusion: Articles earlier than 2015 (unless they contribute historically to the foundation), technical report only or articles on non-peer-reviewed blogs online.
Extraction and Analysis of Key Findings

The researchers reviewed the studies to extract findings based on:

Capabilities of frameworks (performance, scalability, cross-platform).

Pedagogical fit (support for multimedia, interactivity, learner-centeredness).

Comparative data derived (ways in which frameworks vary, in educational use).

The findings were thematically coded, with technical dimensions (e.g., speed, security, maintainability) and pedagogical dimensions (e.g., usability, accessibility, multimedia).

Research Questions

Based on the review of capabilities and pedagogical fit, the following research questions (RQ) inform this paper:

RQ1 - How do Java-based frameworks (Spring Boot, Jakarta EE) meet the technical and pedagogical needs of language learning applications?

RQ2 - How do .NET frameworks (ASP.NET Core, Blazor) differ from Java frameworks regarding scalability, multimedia integration, and usability for educational applications?

RQ3 - How do new alternatives (Python and Django, Flask, Node.js, React, Angular) differ from each other regarding developer productivity, adaptation, and affordances for language learning applications?

RQ4 - What framework features predict successful implementation of multimedia-rich language-learning applications? 

RQ5 - What gaps remain in the literature on frameworks and the selection of measurable language learning outcomes?
These inquiries promote a review that extends beyond the technical to educational impact and in the context of the UoN Language Learning Web Application study.
